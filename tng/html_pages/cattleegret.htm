Cattle Egret

While still a rare bird, after the first ever UK breeding in Somerset, a Cattle Egret turned up at the res in 2009.

13/10/09 - A Cattle Egret was perched in a dead tree in the SE corner this evening when I arrived at the hide at 18:00, but amazing timing....3 hot-air balloons flew over & spooked everything....the Egret flew northwards & climbed up over the trees on the north boundary....I thought it was gone, but it circled back & landed in the Scots Pines in the NE corner! Andy & I arrived at the dam end & had good views of the settled bird. It later flew to a dead tree by the reedbed at 18:50, when I left (DWH, AG).

27/11/09 - presumed the same bird as above returned to roost (DWH)

21/02/17 - A single bird seen for just a few moments, but photographed (SH,KGH). Also then seen on 24/02, 25/02, 26/02, 27/02, 01/03 and 02/03

18/09/18 - A single bird photographed by the finder (SH)

31/07/20 - A single adult in full breeding plumage (SH,DWH)

09/09/20 - A single bird (SH) (no breeding colour) with further sightings on 10/09, 11/09, 13/09 and 16/09, with possibly 2 different birds involved

11/07/25 - A single bird (DWH) in breeding colour flew in at dusk and stayed for a further 2 days
