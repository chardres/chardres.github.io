2011 sightings

2011 - A fairly slow start to the year, but Goosanders were the stars of January and February right through to the begining of March. Common elsewhere, 3 Gadwall were nice to see in February. Siskin numbers were good in early March with 20+ birds. A singing Mistle Thrush was heard on March 9th and the same day recorded the earliest ever Sand Martin for Chard. By the end of March a couple of Swallows had been seen and both Chiffchaff and Blackcap had been heard singing, On May 31st there was the first Chard record of Egyptian Goose. Another first believe it or not was Linnet on August 5th. Presumably no one bothered to record Linnet in the past? The same day also saw the second record of Crossbill. 5 pairs of Great Crested Grebes are believed to have bred this year and 4 nestboxes were used by Nuthatches. It was a good year for Terns with up to 10 Common Terns together in August, Black Terns were seen on 5 dates and 2 Common Terns and a Sandwich Tern gave a show on September 2nd/3rd. If rarities are more your thing, October brought several Mediterranean Gulls and a Spoonbill. Then December provided 2 very nice male Ring-necked Ducks and another Mediterranean Gull. And finally a Yellow-Legged Gull on the last day of the year.
