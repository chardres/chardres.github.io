September 1st 2004

  • Little Egret 1
  • Grey Heron 3
  • Common Sandpiper 2
  • Kingfisher 1
  • Chiffchaff 1+
  • Warbler sp. 1

 

A bulky yellow/green warbler present today. Very yellow breast like young Willow Warbler yet larger and bulkier. My first thought was Melodious Warbler, but not good enough views for me to tell for sure. I ruled out Icterine Warbler on the basis of leg colour (brownish) and no pale wing panel.

Update - James Packer suggests that this bird could be a Wood Warbler and I have to agree with this. I didn't consider the Phylloscs seriously due to the big size difference between this bird and Chiffchaff, but actually Wood Warbler is nearer in size to Melodious than it is to Willow/Chiff.

I didn't see the white belly that usually gives a Wood Warbler away and the skulking in bushes behaviour is not typical for Wood Warblers, but not unknown on migration. I did notice very contrasty tertials and this is a feature of Wood Warblers, though not wrong for a young Melodious.

As a Wood Warbler it would be the 3rd record for Chard Res (Whereas Melodious would be the 5th for Somerset and first inland record).

In conclusion it is much more likely to be a Wood Warbler than a Melodious - Thanks James.
