2013 Review

It's only when you look back through the records that you realise what a good year it was for birds at Chard, especially passage birds. A Goldeneye on January 15th was most welcome as was a record count of 33 Snipe on 25th, a Greylag on 28th and a Goosander on 31st. Mediterranean Gulls were good to see throughout the year from February onwards. There was an amazing fall of 80+ Chiffchaffs on March 23rd which for an inland site is so surprising. April, May and June were fairly quiet. Waders were very well represented for a change starting with a Redshank and 12 Common Sandpipers in late July. Then August started with a Greenshank, Black-tailed Godwit and the 3rd ever record of Wood Sandpiper on 8th. 2 more Greenshank on 10th, another Redshank on 13th, 15 Green Sandpipers (record count) and another Greenshank on 15th. The 16th brought 14 Green Sandpipers, a Black-tailed Godwit , a Greenshank and 7 Common Sandpipers. The 18th added another couple of Redshanks and then a Ringed Plover turned up on 23rd. That would have been enough waders for the month, but another Wood Sandpiper turned up on 28th. On the 2nd September yet another Wood Sandpiper was seen, the 6th brought a Knot (2nd record) and on the 10th a Little Stint (3rd record). A Dunlin on 12th would have finished this off nicely, but there was still one surprise left with a Grey Phalarope (3rd record) on 18th. Crossbills (4th record) were seen on 21st September, a Great White Egret on 25th and just in case we thought the waders had finished, there was a Little Ringed Plover on 29th that stayed for nearly a week. Another Great White Egret on October 7th should have wrapped up the rarities for the year, but the very same day a Yellow-browed Warbler (1st record) was found by Roger Harris. A record count of 187 Canada Geese, 4 Mediterranean Gulls and a Water Rail were a noisy end to the month. November saw 10+ Mandarins, a Mediterranean Gull and a Goosander. December wasn't devoid of birds either with a Pochard (not so common now), a couple of Mediterranean Gulls, a Great White Egret and 2 Egyptian Geese (2nd Record). A Mistle Thrush singing on 25th rounded off the year nicely. There are other birds I haven't mentioned like Peregrine, Raven and Common Tern, but this year I don't have space to include all the good birds in the review.
Kevin Harris
