2010 sightings

2010 wasn't a match on 2009 for new birds, but there were a few surprises, and some good numbers of scarce birds caused by the very cold weather of December. For a start there were Goosanders seen on a remarkable 60 days and the count of 22 Wigeon was the best for 13 years. In October there was a record count of 11 exotic Mandarin ducks. The cold weather helped to push the Coot count up to 33, the highest for 16 years. The first Chard record of Mediterranean Gull was in 1992 but this year there were sightings on 7 dates over 3 months. Another bird pushed by the cold weather was Skylark. A single bird in October was followed by records on 5 dates in December including 7 together. There were only 2 previous Skylark records in all the years put together. An elusive Lesser Spotted Woodpecker made a welcome return in January after an absence of 6 years. A big surprise was a Wheatear on the shoreline in September, only the 2nd record for the res. A Firecrest stayed for over 2 weeks in January, long enough for even me to find it eventually. Of the breeding birds it was great to hear that no less than 4 nestboxes were occupied by Nuthatches. Lastly, 6 Reed Buntings together in December doesn't sound like many but it was the highest count for 10 years.
