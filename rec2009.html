2009 sightings

New for the reservoir in 2009 were: Great White Egret, Cattle Egret and Whooper Swan. There was a near miss too with Golden Plover in nearby fields. Also we had the 2nd record of Spotted Redshank, 3rd and 4th records of both Little Ringed Plover and Turnstone. Not forgetting that Red Throated Diver and Mandarin Duck recorded their 6th ever records as well to make a fantastic autumn. Added to that is Firecrest, the first for 10 years.

Another first for the Res and another Egret. If only the Great White had still been around. In Dave Helliar's own words:

A Cattle Egret was perched in a dead tree in the SE corner this evening when I arrived at the hide at 18:00, but amazing timing....3 hot-air balloons flew over & spooked everything....the Egret flew northwards & climbed up over the trees on the north boundary....I thought it was gone, but it circled back & landed in the Scots Pines in the NE corner! Andy & I arrived at the dam end & had good views of the settled bird. It later flew to a dead tree by the reedbed at 18:50, when I left.

Also... A whole bunch of what I believe to be Hornets were around the entrance to the wood today.
