2016 sightings

2016 review

Gadwall were first recorded on 2nd Jan and seen on a record 79 dates through the year with up to 8 birds, The Gadwall are staying around now for extended periods of time which they never used to do. A pair of Mute Swans started the year with 5 young, but one was very ill and was put down. A Kingfisher was noted from the start of the year and there was a high January count of 44 Tufted Duck. An adult Med Gull completed the month on 28th January. Little Egrets were noticeable early in the year with a peak of 18 on 3rd February. February was also good for Med Gulls with 4 adults present on 4th. 2 Redpoll were spotted in the woods and a party of 8 Siskins found their way from local gardens to the res in February. Another Med Gull was seen in March and the first migrants came with 2 Sand Martins on 13th March and Swallows arriving before the end of the month. Even the first House Martins arrived by 1st April. There was a record count of 350+ Common Gulls on 7th April and a Greylag Goose was an unusual visitor towards the end of the month. Spotted Flycatchers were first seen in April and stayed to breed. A mystery duck arrived in May (along with another Greylag) and was thought to be a hybrid, with Red Crested Pochard as one possible parent. In June a Common Tern was seen on the same day as the fishing was closed due to a large number of Carp deaths. Another Common Tern was also seen a week later. In July, 4 Great Crested Grebe chicks were seen and another single older juvenile. These were the only grebe young to be raised this year. The female of the Mute Swan pair was sadly put down in July with a severe sinus infection. The male continued to look after the 4 young until 20th November when he disappeared. August produced a good number of interesting birds with Black-tailed Godwit, Lapwing, Redshank, Greenshank, Mediterranean Gull, Black Tern, Hobby, Little Ringed Plover and a Great White Egret. Also a Wheatear was seen just outside the recording area in Oaklands Avenue. The waders stayed into September with 5 species of wader present on the 5th. A Dunlin and Med Gull also brightened up September. October brought another 2 Med Gulls, a passing Hobby, a Peregrine and a White-fronted Goose (first record since the 1970's). A young Cormorant with a red ring proved to have come from Holland and another with an Orange ring joined it in November. The Orange ringed bird had come from the exact opposite direction in Cornwall. The all time record count of Great Crested Grebes was broken 2 days following in November with a high of 72 birds. A colour ringed Black-headed Gull had come from Hamburg and would likely have passed the very area where the dutch Cormorant originated. In December a Goldeneye appeared on a misty day and stayed throughout the month. To round off the year a Redshank was seen on 29th December and was the first ever December record.
